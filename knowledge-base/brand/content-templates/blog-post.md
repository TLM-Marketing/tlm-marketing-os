# Blog Post Template

## Format Guidelines
- Length: 800-1,500 words (long enough to be useful, short enough to finish)
- Tone: Educational and generous. Teach the reader something they can use today.
- Structure: Problem → Why it exists → How to fix it → How Flowdeck helps (light touch)
- Product mention: Keep it to the last 10-15% of the post. Lead with value, not pitch.
- Include a meta description (under 160 characters) for SEO.
- Use H2 and H3 headers to break up sections.
- Include at least one concrete example, number, or framework.

## Template Structure

```
# [TITLE — specific, benefit-oriented, under 70 characters]

**Meta description:** [Under 160 characters — what the reader will learn]

## The Problem
[2-3 paragraphs describing the problem the reader faces. Be specific. Use scenarios they recognize.]

## Why This Happens
[2-3 paragraphs explaining the root cause. This is where you show expertise — go beyond the obvious.]

## How to Fix It
[The actionable part. A framework, checklist, or step-by-step approach. Use subheaders, bullets, or numbered lists.]

### Step 1: [Action]
[Explanation + example]

### Step 2: [Action]
[Explanation + example]

### Step 3: [Action]
[Explanation + example]

## How Flowdeck Helps
[1-2 short paragraphs connecting the solution to Flowdeck. Don't hard-sell — just show how the product fits into the approach you just described.]

## Key Takeaway
[2-3 sentences summarizing the main insight. What should the reader remember?]

---
**CTA:** [What to do next — book a demo, download a guide, try it free]
```

## Example Outline

**Title:** Why Your Lead Routing Is Costing You Pipeline

**Meta description:** Most mid-market teams lose 20-30% of qualified leads to slow or broken routing. Here's how to fix it.

**The Problem:** Leads come in from 5 different sources. Each one hits a different tool. By the time the SDR gets the lead, 4 hours have passed and the prospect already booked a demo with a competitor.

**Why This Happens:** Most teams built their routing one integration at a time. Each new channel added a new Zapier chain. Nobody ever stepped back to design the full workflow.

**How to Fix It:**
1. Map every lead source to a single entry point
2. Define routing rules based on lead score + intent signals, not just form fills
3. Set SLA alerts — if a lead isn't touched in 15 minutes, escalate

**How Flowdeck Helps:** Flowdeck's workflow builder lets you define routing rules visually, connect every lead source, and set automatic SLA alerts. Average response time drops from 4 hours to 12 minutes.

**Key Takeaway:** Lead routing isn't a "set it and forget it" setup. It's the most important workflow in your GTM stack. Treat it like one.

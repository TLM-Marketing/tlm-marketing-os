# LinkedIn Post Template

## Format Guidelines
- Length: 150-300 words (short enough to read in the feed, long enough to say something real)
- First line: Hook — the reader decides in 2 seconds whether to keep reading. Make it count.
- Structure: Hook → Context → Insight → CTA
- Use line breaks generously — walls of text get scrolled past
- End with a question or clear CTA to drive engagement
- Hashtags: 3-5 max, placed at the end

## Template Structure

```
[HOOK — one provocative line that stops the scroll]

[CONTEXT — 2-3 sentences setting up the problem or situation]

[INSIGHT — the main point. What did you learn? What should the reader know?]

[PROOF — a number, example, or real story that backs up the insight]

[CTA — a question to the audience, or a clear next step]

[HASHTAGS — 3-5 relevant hashtags]
```

## Example Post

Most marketing teams are running 5+ tools to do one job.

One for email. One for ads. One for lead scoring. One for attribution. One for... you get it.

The problem isn't the tools. It's the gaps between them.

Every time a lead moves from one tool to another, you lose context. The SDR doesn't know which campaign brought them in. The CMO can't tell the board what generated pipeline.

We see this pattern constantly with mid-market teams:
→ Great tools individually
→ Zero visibility end-to-end

The fix isn't adding another tool. It's replacing the duct tape with a system.

What's the most frustrating "gap" in your GTM stack?

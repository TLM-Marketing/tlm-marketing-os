# Competitive Analysis

TLM's site doesn't name specific competitors, so the entries below are category-level alternatives, grounded in the "Why Choose TLM?" positioning. The sales/marketing team should refine with named competitors as they come up in deals.

## Competitors

### Traditional B2B marketing agencies
**Strengths:** Established brand/creative or paid-media capabilities; familiar engagement model.
**Weaknesses:** Activity-first (deliverables, not pipeline); rarely AI-native; siloed inbound vs. outbound; vendor relationship, not shared KPIs.
**How TLM positions:** Revenue-first, full-funnel under one roof, AI-native, and accountable to one KPI — pipeline. "We don't follow playbooks — we rewrite them."

### In-house marketing teams
**Strengths:** Deep product/context knowledge; full control.
**Weaknesses:** Slow and costly to scale; hard to hire AI-native GTM talent; tool sprawl and cost.
**How TLM positions:** Full in-house team impact at a fraction of the cost; quick ramp-up; third-party tool costs included; AI GTM Engineering that builds the engine and transfers it back — no new hires.

### Point solutions & intent/ABM tools (e.g., 6sense, ZoomInfo, Clay-style stacks)
**Strengths:** Strong data or single-function capability.
**Weaknesses:** Just a tool — leaves strategy, execution, and integration to the customer; signals stuck in dashboards.
**How TLM positions:** Turns dark-funnel intent into Sales Accepted Meetings — execution and workflows included, not just a dashboard. Tools, tech, and expertise all handled for you.

### Offshore SDR / outbound shops
**Strengths:** Low-cost outbound volume.
**Weaknesses:** Disconnected from inbound and brand; generic messaging; no full-funnel view.
**How TLM positions:** SDR-level output at half the cost, but integrated with inbound and analytics, triggered by real-time intent, with messaging that drives replies.
